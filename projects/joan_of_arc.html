Joan of Arc: Last Prophecy A personal Unreal Engine 5 project

A dark fantasy open world born from France’s medieval myths, divine voices, and the chaos of war.

Concept & Project Vision

Platform: Unreal Engine 5 (C++ & Blueprint)

Joan of Arc: Last Prophecy is an open-world action RPG developed to strengthen my skills in C++ programming, Blueprint scripting, and Unreal level design by integrating Nanite for real-time rendering of high-polygon assets with automatic LOD handling.

Inspiration

The project draws inspiration from France’s historical and cultural heritage, centered on one of its most iconic figures — Joan of Arc, the young peasant girl who led the French army during the Hundred Years’ War before her capture and execution in 1431.

Dark Fantasy

Rather than a purely historical simulation, the concept blends authentic history with dark fantasy, inspired by French medieval folklore, myths, and superstitions. This semi-historical, semi-fictional approach provides creative freedom in storytelling, art direction, and gameplay — where divine voices, witchcraft, and mythical creatures coexist within a grounded medieval world.

Narrative Direction & Game Design Document

The narrative explores faith, war, and destiny through a grounded yet mystical retelling of Joan’s journey. The Game Design Document (GDD) defines the core narrative structure, gameplay systems, and artistic direction.

  • Duality between light and darkness (faith vs corruption)
  • Mythical reinterpretation of real historical events
  • Integration of French folklore and religious mysticism into worldbuilding

Core Gameplay Development

Developed from scratch in C++ and Blueprint, the project currently includes the foundations of a third-person action RPG:

  • Third-person character controller (movement, sprint, crouch, jump and camera behavior)
  • Player stats: health, stamina, experience, and level progression
  • Basic HUD integration

Next features under development :

  • Combat system (melee, parry, and magic abilities)
  • Dialogue and NPC interaction system
  • AI for enemies and creatures
  • Quest and equipment systems
  • Faith-based and magical ability mechanics
  • Audio and ambient design integration

Level Design

The first playable area is currently being built using Quixel Megascans, focusing on realistic medieval architecture and atmosphere.
